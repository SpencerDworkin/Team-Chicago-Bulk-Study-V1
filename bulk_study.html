A-01Site Plan & Setbacks

Plan view showing the existing 1.08-acre site, the footprint of the seven-story structure, the Addison CTA Red Line stop at the southeast corner of the parcel, and the relationship to Wrigley Field across W. Addison Street. All setbacks comply with the governing PD ordinance; no zoning variances are in effect.

A-01 · Site Plan & Setbacks NTS · Dimensions Approximate
Key Observation The site achieves ~95% lot coverage — typical of urban infill TOD development. The governing PD ordinance permits 0'–8' setbacks on all frontages, reflecting the continuous streetwall character of the Clark Street corridor. The Addison CTA Red Line stop is approximately 320 feet from the building entrance, qualifying the site for TOD parking reductions under the Chicago Zoning Ordinance.

A-02Vertical Massing — West Elevation

Elevation from N. Clark Street showing the seven-floor stacking. The podium (Floors 1–3) is setback 0' from Clark and presents a continuous retail streetwall; the residential tower (Floors 4–7) is modestly setback at the 4th-floor terrace to reduce apparent massing and accommodate the amenity deck. Overall building height is below the 90' maximum permitted under the governing PD.

A-02 · Vertical Massing Diagram NTS · Heights Approximate
Key Observation The podium/tower massing strategy preserves the traditional streetwall character of the Clark Street retail corridor while tucking the residential floors behind a 5-foot setback at the 4th floor. The resulting composition mitigates apparent bulk from the pedestrian plane and creates the open-air amenity deck that serves the residential units. Total building height is approximately 88 feet, below the governing PD maximum.

A-04FAR & Bulk Compliance

The Property was delivered under a Planned Development (PD) ordinance — the City of Chicago's negotiated zoning tool for large mixed-use projects that exceed baseline B3-5 entitlements. Under the PD, allowable bulk is established by the ordinance itself rather than the underlying district; the building as-delivered is fully compliant with the terms of the governing PD.

Underwriting Implication The building's delivered FAR of ~10.97 represents essentially full utilization of the PD envelope. This supports a reversionary / stabilized cap-rate valuation approach rather than a land-residual approach.

A-05Parking Ratio & TOD Compliance

The Property is located within a Chicago-designated Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) zone — within one-quarter mile of the Addison CTA Red Line station, a high-frequency rapid-transit stop. TOD designation permits material reductions in required on-site parking for residential uses, and the Property was approved with a residential parking ratio substantially below the baseline B3 district standard.

Revenue Concentration of Commercial Parking

The 329-space commercial parking facility is the only proximate structured parking serving the Wrigleyville entertainment district, generating approximately $4,947 per space annually from a mix of Cubs gameday demand, Wrigley Field event parking, retail customer parking, and neighborhood visitor parking. Commercial parking contributes approximately 18.7% of combined Property NOI and is addressed as a concentration risk in the Key Risks & Mitigants section of the Credit Committee Memorandum.

Zoning Conclusion The Property is an existing, fully entitled, stabilized asset. All setbacks, FAR, height, density, and parking ratios as-built conform to the governing PD ordinance; no rezoning, variances, or massing modifications are required in connection with the proposed financing. The drawings in this bulk study (A-01 through A-05) document this conformance and satisfy the Credit Committee Memorandum's zoning & massing deliverable.
